Congress initially encouraged litigation over prison conditions
by enactment in 1980 of the Civil Rights of Institutionalized Per-
sons Act,260 but then in 1996 added restrictions through enactment
of the Prison Litigation Reform Act.261 The Court upheld the latter
law’s provision for an automatic stay of prospective relief upon the
ﬁling of a motion to modify or terminate that relief, ruling that sepa-
ration of powers principles were not violated.262
