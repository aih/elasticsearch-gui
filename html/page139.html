VOTER (ELECTOR) QUALIFICATIONS
It was the original constitutional scheme to vest the determina-
tion of qualiﬁcations for voters or “electors” in congressional elec-
tions 305 solely in the discretion of the states, save only for the ex-
press requirement that the states could prescribe no qualiﬁcations
other than those provided for voters for the more numerous branch
of the legislature.306 This language has never been expressly changed,
but the discretion of the states—and not only with regard to the
qualiﬁcations of congressional electors—has long been circum-
scribed by express constitutional limitations found in various con-
stitutional amendments 307 and by the Equal Protection Clause.308
And, since the right to vote for United States Representatives is
conceptually derived from the Federal Constitution,309 Congress has
the power under Article I, § 4, to legislate to protect that right against
both official 310 and private denial.311
