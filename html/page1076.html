In any event, following ratiﬁcation, Madison in his successful campaign for a seat
in the House ﬁrmly endorsed the proposal of a bill of rights. “[I]t is my sincere opinion
that the Constitution ought to be revised, and that the ﬁrst Congress meeting under
it ought to prepare and recommend to the States for ratiﬁcation, the most satisfac-
tory provisions for all essential rights, particularly the rights of Conscience in the full-
est latitude, the freedom of the press, trials by jury, security against general warrants
& c.” 5 THE WRITINGS OF JAMES MADISON 319 (G. Hunt ed., 1904).
