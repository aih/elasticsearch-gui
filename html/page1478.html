long as we have had the exclusionary rule, critics have attacked it,
challenged its premises, disputed its morality.454 By the early 1980s,
a majority of Justices had stated a desire either to abolish the rule
or to sharply curtail its operation,455 and numerous opinions had
rejected all doctrinal bases other than deterrence.456 At the same
