Enemy Aliens.—The Alien Enemy Act of 1798 authorized the
President to deport any alien or to license him to reside within the
United States at any place to be designated by the President.1646
Though critical of the measure, many persons conceded its constitu-
tionality on the theory that Congress’ power to declare war carried
with it the power to treat the citizens of a foreign power against
which war has been declared as enemies entitled to summary jus-
tice.1647 A similar statute was enacted during World War I 1648 and
was held valid in Ludecke v. Watkins.1649
